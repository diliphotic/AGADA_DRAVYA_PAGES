स्नुही (SNUHI)

Latin Name: Euphorbia neriifolia
Family: Euphorbiaceae

Irritant poison classified under Upavisha and Sthavara Visha in Ayurveda.

Introduction

स्नुही is a drastic purgative. It is found in rocky soil throughout India as small trees. It is one among the upavisha and categorised under sthavara visha. A succulent plant known for its milky latex, classified as a semi-poisonous plant in Ayurveda. Used in Ayurveda for treating skin diseases, piles and abdominal issues.

Morphology

Habit – Large erect glabrous shrub up to 20 feet.

Leaves – Small fleshy rectangular, clustered at branch tips.

Stem – Cylindrical, green, fleshy with spiral ridges and sharp spines.

Flowers – Small yellow-green arranged in cyme.

Fruits – Small 3-lobed capsule.

Latex – Thick milky white, medicinally used.

Seeds – Smooth greenish brown, mustard sized.

Toxicological Profile

Poisonous Part
Milky Latex
Fatal Dose
125–250 mg
Fatal Period
12–24 hours

Mode of Action

Snuhi Latex → Diterpenoid esters → Protein Kinase‑C activation → Inflammatory mediator release → Irritation, vesication & tissue necrosis

Signs & Symptoms

Skin: Burning sensation, vesication, dermatitis, inflammation.
Eyes: Severe pain, lacrimation, conjunctivitis, keratitis, blurred vision.
Internal: Vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, convulsions, coma.

व्रण, शोथ, आध्मान, मूर्च्छा, प्रलाप, उदर दाह

Treatment

Ayurveda
Buffalo milk, Vamana, Virechana, Cow’s milk with sugar, Draksha rasa
Modern
Wash affected area and symptomatic treatment
